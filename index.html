Message

From the Chairman...

Oct 07, 2023

The Delhi Urban Art Commission (DUAC) was established by an Act of the Parliament in 1973 to "advise the Government of India in the matter of preserving, developing, and maintaining the aesthetic quality of urban and environmental design within Delhi and to provide advice and guidance to any local body in respect of any project of building operations or engineering operations or any development proposal which affects or is likely to affect the skyline or the aesthetic quality of the surroundings or any public amenity provided therein.” The Commission is entrusted with three roles (i) Policy Advisor to the Government of India, (ii) Regulatory

Body and (iii) Think Tank. As the DUAC approaches its 50th anniversary, the relevance of the institution has never been greater. The mandate of preserving, developing, and maintaining the aesthetic quality of Delhi’s urban and environmental design is being debated by more of Delhi’s and the nation’s inhabitants than ever before. In recent years the DUAC has delivered enabling and foundational blocks that will serve to improve delivery on its mandate: (i) Prepared the Unified Building Bye Laws (UBBL 2016) for Delhi to contemporize bye laws set in 1983, (ii) Prepared the draft of the Public Art Policy and Guidelines, (iii) Streamlined the process for DUAC approvals by commissioning the Online Proposal Assessment & Approval System (OPAAS), (iv) Prepared draft Lutyens Bungalow Zone (LBZ ) guidelines in 2019, and (v) Recommended Dynamic Parking Norms (DPN) that streamline many of the anomalies in vehicular parking in Delhi. To address criticism of arbitrariness and subjectivity in DUAC decisions on the large number of proposals that come to it for approval, criteria was developed and placed on the DUAC website for project proponents to become aware of what the DUAC is looking for. This brings greater objectivity, reduces uncertainty, and helps architects make comprehensive submissions. Aesthetics remains a matter of subjectivity but together we can ensure that quality of proposals improve and proportion of rejections are minimized. The DUAC intends to catalyze public discourse on relevant subjects to enhance the quality of submissions. Over the past decade, the DUAC has conducted 77 site-specific studies to address specific problems in the city. The Commission looks forward to finding the method and means to implementing the best of these in its current tenure. Three personal priorities are (i) Improving the quality and useability of green areas, (ii) Implementing a sustainable model for upgrading currently informal urban development, and (iii) Renewing the water bodies and waterways of the city while improving water resource management. Delhi is poised to become the world’s largest urban agglomeration this decade. With your active support we can also make it the most desirable urban destination for residents, workers, and tourists within the same time frame. We look forward to your ideas and support to provide Delhi a cleaner, greener, and more beautiful tomorrow!
Sincerely,
Ajit Pai
Chairman, Delhi Urban Art Commission
